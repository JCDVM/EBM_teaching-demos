P-values and the probability of replication

If you ran this exact study again — same sample size, same variability, true effect equal to what you observed — what's the chance you'd get p<0.05 a second time?

Observed p-value: 0.0500  (implied z = 1.96)
Probability an exact replication also hits p<0.05: 50%

This is a best-case number: it assumes the original estimate is exactly right and the replication is run under identical conditions. It ignores publication bias, effect-size inflation in small or novel studies (the "winner's curse"), and any heterogeneity between the original and replication samples — all of which push real-world replication rates lower than this curve suggests.
